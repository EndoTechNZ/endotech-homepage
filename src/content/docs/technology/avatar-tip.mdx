## Why the Apical Third Matters Most

The apical third of the root canal represents the highest-risk zone during instrumentation. This is where:

- Canal curvature is often most severe
- Dentinal walls are thinnest
- The apical constriction must be preserved
- Procedural errors have the greatest biological consequences

**Precision at the terminus defines success.**

## The Avatar Tip Design

Avatar Tip is a precision-engineered apical geometry designed to enhance control in these critical millimeters. Its refined transition profile promotes smooth advancement while minimising risk.

## Clinical Evidence: The Optimal Zone

Histologic research by Ricucci and Siqueira (2011) examined apical limit outcomes in teeth with necrotic pulps and apical periodontitis.

### Key Findings

| Termination Point | Clinical Outcome |
|-------------------|------------------|
| 0.5–1.5 mm short of apex | **Highest healing rates** |
| At or beyond apex | Persistent periapical inflammation |
| >2 mm short of apex | Significantly reduced success |

> "Highest healing rates observed at 0.5–1.5 mm short of the apical foramen. Overfilling associated with persistent periapical inflammation."
>
> — Ricucci D, Siqueira JF Jr. *Oral Surgery, Oral Medicine, Oral Pathology, Oral Radiology, and Endodontology*. 2011;112(6):825–842.

### Clinical Implication

Technical precision at the apical third materially affects biological healing. The Avatar Tip is designed to support controlled termination within this healing-favourable zone.

## Reducing Iatrogenic Risk

During instrumentation of curved canals, files exhibit elastic restoring forces that drive lateral wall pressure and potential transportation. These forces increase as curvature severity increases.

The Avatar Tip mitigates these mechanical drivers through:

- **Reduced engagement force** — smoother initial contact
- **Maintained centring** — throughout curved anatomy
- **Anatomical respect** — preserves apical constriction integrity
