VortiFlow Irrigation Needle is presented as an engineered irrigation delivery concept positioned after shaping and before final sealing.

Rather than treating the needle as a commodity accessory, the VortiFlow page frames irrigation delivery around outlet geometry, flow direction, and workflow placement within the wider EndoTech system.

## Clinical Problem

Chemical debridement depends on fluid reaching prepared canal space, exchanging effectively, and supporting debris transport without over-reliance on direct apical jetting.

That means irrigation is not only about irrigant selection. It is also about how the delivery device influences:

- depth of placement
- direction of flow
- replenishment of fresh solution
- debris transport
- procedural control near the apical region

## Design Logic

VortiFlow is described around:

- helical or spiral channels on the needle wall
- lateral irrigant exit
- an internal flow path not directly open at the tip
- a smooth, closed-end tip concept
- intended vortex-like flow behaviour within the prepared canal

The page uses this design logic to explain why fluid movement can be influenced by needle geometry rather than by volume alone.

## Workflow Placement

Within the EndoTech system story, VortiFlow sits between shaping and sealing:

- Acrobat
- TransformX
- VortiFlow
- BCS / iRoot

That positioning keeps the irrigation step connected to the canal form created during shaping and the sealing objectives that follow.
