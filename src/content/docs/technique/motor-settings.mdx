## Quick Reference

TransformX files are designed to work with your existing motor settings. No changes required from your current workflow.

---

## ET TransformX Settings

For clinicians using EdgeTaper, EdgeX7, Race Evo, or similar .04/.06 taper systems.

| Step | File | Speed | Torque | Purpose |
|------|------|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Acrobat 13/.03 | 300 RPM | 2.0–2.5 Ncm | Glide Path |
| 2 | ET 20/.04 | 350 RPM | 3.0 Ncm | Shaping |
| 3 | ET 25/.04 | 350 RPM | 3.0 Ncm | Finishing |

:::tip[Speed Range]
ET files can run at speeds up to **500 RPM** if preferred. Use your clinical judgement based on canal anatomy.
:::

### ET Sequence Summary
1. **Glide Path** — Establish with Acrobat to working length
2. **Shape** — ET 20/.04 to working length
3. **Finish** — ET 25/.04 to working length (or larger as anatomy dictates)

**Between every file:** Irrigate → Recapitulate → Re-Irrigate

## PT TransformX Settings

For clinicians using ProTaper Gold or ProTaper Next systems.

| Step | File | Speed | Torque | Purpose |
|------|------|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | SX | 300 RPM | 3.0 Ncm | Coronal Shaping |
| 2 | S1, S2 | 300 RPM | 3.0 Ncm | Middle Third Shaping |
| 3 | F1, F2, F3 | 300 RPM | 3.0 Ncm | Apical Finishing |

### PT Sequence Summary
1. **Coronal** — SX for initial coronal flaring
2. **Shape** — S1 then S2 to working length
3. **Finish** — F1, F2, or F3 based on apical anatomy

**Between every file:** Irrigate → Recapitulate → Re-Irrigate

## General Guidelines

### Speed Settings
- All TransformX files operate safely within standard rotary speed ranges (300–500 RPM)
- Use the speed you're comfortable with from your current system
- Lower speeds offer more tactile feedback; higher speeds may improve cutting efficiency

### Torque Settings
- Recommended torque: **2.0–3.0 Ncm** depending on file size
- Auto-reverse/auto-stop torque control is recommended
- Trust your motor's feedback—if it's binding, back out and re-irrigate

### Key Principles

1. **Establish glide path first** — Always confirm patency before rotary instrumentation
2. **Let the file work** — Avoid excessive apical pressure
3. **Irrigate frequently** — Between every file, minimum
4. **Recapitulate** — Use a small hand file to confirm patency
5. **Inspect files** — Discard any showing signs of fatigue
